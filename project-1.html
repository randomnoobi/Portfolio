How it works

Desk Talk

Desk Talk is a student project that uses computer vision and a chat-style interface to make your desk feel more organized, aware, and helpful. It notices everyday desk situations like spills, clutter, missing items, posture, and focus distractions, then turns them into calm reminders.

Main function

Manage your desk in a simpler, smarter way.

Every day, we spend a lot of time around the desk. On the desk, we have computers, cups, keys, books, and daily objects. But these objects are not being actively maintained or kept organized, which can lead to chaos like lost keys and water spills.

Desk Talk uses a camera and a chat-style interface to track what is on your desk and turn everyday objects into gentle reminders that keep you organized and focused. It can warn you about spills or clutter, remind you to take breaks, and help you find misplaced items.

Three scenes

What Desk Talk watches for

Each scene matches a real desk moment. The product focuses on simple, useful situations: risks near your computer, lost objects, and habits that build up over time.

01

Hazards near your computer

Water or risky items too close to the machine, pets on the desk, or clutter crowding your screen zone — you get a clear heads-up before damage or distraction happens.

02

Your things, accounted for

Headphones, phone, keys, and other small items appear on your desk — Desk Talk helps you notice what is present, what moved, and what might be missing.

03

Habits that add up

Posture checks, sitting too close to the screen, drinking water, and watering plants — light nudges tied to how you actually use your desk.

Technical pipeline

How it works

Three pipelines run behind the chat: one watches for physical risk, one keeps a light memory of where your things were, and one turns habits into useful reminders.

Technical pipeline

How the dialogue works

The dialogue system connects user messages, object-specific characters, memory, idle conversations, and danger detection. This lets Desk Talk respond like a small group of desk agents instead of a normal notification system.

Why it matters

More than notifications.

Most tools shout at you. Desk Talk is built around the idea that your desk already tells a story — objects, distance, light habits. When that story is translated into a calm chat, you get fewer surprises, less damage, and a workspace that feels intentional instead of chaotic.

Under the hood: computer vision for what is on the desk, lightweight rules for risk and presence, and a conversational layer so every alert feels human-sized — not a dashboard, not a siren.
